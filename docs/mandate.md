# DISPATCH — SYSTEM MANDATE v1

**Directed Intelligence for Strategic Positioning Across Technology, Culture & Healthcare**

---

## 1. PURPOSE

Dispatch is a personal intelligence system for Jeremy Grant. It continuously monitors the information environment that shapes his career positioning and strategic decision-making. It processes signal from text and audio sources across five intelligence layers, synthesizes patterns across domains, and maintains a conversational advisor (Cerebro) that connects insight to action.

Dispatch does not filter the world down to a handful of headlines. It makes the entire corpus of relevant intelligence legible, navigable, and actionable for a single human on a mission. Every signal is available. Nothing is hidden. The system provides architecture — scoring, layering, cross-referencing — so the operator can move through the full body of knowledge with intent.

## 3. THE FIVE INTELLIGENCE LAYERS

Every signal Dispatch processes is evaluated through five lenses. These are not categories — they are layers of relevance that can overlap. A single article can score high on multiple layers simultaneously, and those multi-layer signals are the most valuable.

### Layer 1: THE OPPORTUNITY
Healthcare, pharma, and the AI-health intersection. Not limited to Eli Lilly, but Lilly is the current primary target.

**What to track:**
- Pharma digital transformation (successes, failures, investments)

- AI in drug discovery, clinical operations, and care coordination
- Healthcare system design challenges (access, adherence, navigation)
- Specific company intelligence: Lilly, but also J&J, Pfizer, Roche, Novo Nordisk, Anthem, UnitedHealth — anyone building design capability in healthcare
- FDA policy shifts that create new design requirements
- Health tech startups reshaping care delivery

**Key context:**
- Lilly: 51M patients, $80-83B projected 2026 revenue, $1B NVIDIA AI partnership, LillyDirect pharmacy platform, donanemab approval

### Layer 2: THE POSITION
Jeremy's career trajectory specifically. Hiring signals, compensation benchmarks, interview intelligence, competitive positioning, and the talent dynamics of design leadership.

**What to track:**
- Senior design leadership job postings (CDO, VP Design, Head of Design) — especially in healthcare, pharma, and regulated industries
- Compensation benchmarks and trends for design leadership
- Who is hiring, who is leaving, what the transitions signal
- Agency-to-in-house migration patterns
- Permalance and fractional leadership models
- Interview preparation intelligence: what companies are asking, what they value

### Layer 3: THE DISCIPLINE
How design leadership is evolving as a function. Not Jeremy's career — the profession itself.

**What to track:**
- How the best design organizations are structured
- What CDOs at Fortune 500 companies actually do day-to-day
- Where design authority is expanding (regulated industries, AI governance) vs. contracting (execution automation)
- The design engineering convergence — how it reshapes teams, roles, and value
- AI's impact on design practice: what it automates, what it amplifies, what it can't touch
- Arguments that win board-level investment in design
- Design systems, ops, and infrastructure as organizational capability
- The tools shaping practice: Figma, Cursor, v0, Claude, Vercel, and the platforms on the vanguard

### Layer 4: THE LANDSCAPE
The broader forces that shape the operating environment. Technology, policy, economics, and business model shifts.

**What to track:**

- AI tooling and infrastructure: Claude, Cursor, Vercel, IBM, and others building the next layer
- Technology business model evolution
- Economic signals that affect hiring, investment, and industry direction
- Political and regulatory dynamics that reshape healthcare, tech, and creative industries
- Market movements and venture signals in relevant sectors

### Layer 5: THE CULTURE
Taste, criticism, creative practice, and the intellectual currents that make a design leader worth following. A Head of Design who only reads industry publications is a technician. One who tracks film, architecture, music criticism, and cultural theory can lead creative culture.

**What to track:**
- Architecture, industrial design, and spatial practice
- Film, music, and literary criticism that reveals where attention and craft are heading
- Cultural commentary and essays on technology, society, and creativity
- The creative practices and philosophies of people doing exceptional work
- Emerging aesthetics, movements, and counter-movements

## 5. SIGNAL PROCESSING PRINCIPLES

1. **Relevance is not binary.** Every signal exists on a spectrum from noise to essential. The system scores, it does not hide.

2. **Multi-layer signals are the most valuable.** A pharma AI deal that also speaks to design org structure scores higher than either signal alone. Cross-domain convergence is the highest-value intelligence.

3. **Recency decays.** A signal from today is worth more than the same signal from last week. Urgency scoring reflects this.

4. **Source credibility is weighted.** A Harvard Business Review analysis carries different weight than a TechCrunch headline. An Anthropic research paper carries different weight than a blog post about Anthropic. The system knows the difference.

5. **The whole corpus is available.** Nothing is filtered out. The architecture makes it navigable — scoring, layering, cross-referencing — so the operator can move through the full body with intent.

6. **Everything flows toward Cerebro.** Every view, every signal, every synthesis should have a clear path to the conversational layer. Cerebro is the gravity well of the system.

---

## 6. CEREBRO — THE ADVISOR

Cerebro is Dispatch's conversational intelligence layer. It is not a chatbot. It is a strategic advisor with full context of the operator's mandate, the day's signals, and the historical conversation.

**Operating mode:**
- Trusted senior advisor, not a search engine
- Synthesis first — surface connections the operator might miss
- Name patterns across layers
- Flag noise explicitly — "this doesn't move your needle"
- No bullet points. Tight paragraphs.
- No preamble. Lead with substance.
- No flattery. Clarity over encouragement.
- After every response, push the conversation forward with a follow-up provocation

**Cerebro has access to:**
- The full day's signal feed (text articles, scored and annotated)
- Audio episode metadata (when available)
- Conversation history (persisted across sessions)
- Web search capability for real-time intelligence

**Escalation path:**
When a thread reaches sufficient depth, the operator can escalate to Claude Desktop for extended thinking, Opus-level reasoning, and artifact creation (documents, Google Drive integration). Cerebro provides the on-ramp.

## 7. SYNTHESIS — THE PATTERN LAYER

Synthesis is the right-brain view. It does not show raw data. It shows what the data means.

**Synthesis should answer these questions, 5-10 times a day:**
- What's the most important signal right now, and why?
- How does today's information environment compare to yesterday's?
- Which of my five layers is running hot? Which is cold?
- What cross-layer connections are emerging that I might miss?
- What should I be asking Cerebro about right now?

**Synthesis is not a dashboard.** It is a narrative intelligence layer that presents patterns visually. It should feel like opening a briefing, not checking analytics.
